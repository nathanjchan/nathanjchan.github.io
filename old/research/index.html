Research in Psychology

During the 2017-18 school year, I was a Research Assistant at the Asian American Center on Disparities Research at UC Davis.

The study I worked on was about differences in face concern, or one's own self-image, between Asian Americans and White Americans. As a study confederate, I gave a survey to over 30 participants that gathered data on psychological measures related to emotion and reactions to negative events.

I also gave a test to participants, where they had to come up with as many uses of a brick in three minutes, and gave them fake results, positive or negative. I conducted a video-recorded interview to see how they reacted.

The data set had a sample size of 425 UC Davis students and contained all the survey data. I performed data analysis on it, using ANOVAs and multiple regressions in SPSS.

I designed and wrote a poster titled Ethnic and Gender Differences in Emotion Regulation Strategies: The Mediating Role of Face Concern . The purpose was to investigate how concern for one's own self-image affects reactions to negative events, and how it differs between Asian and White Americans. We found that two emotion regulation strategies (reactions to negative events), self-blame and positive refocusing, were mediated by face concern. More information can be found in the poster and abstract.

I presented the poster at the 2018 Undergraduate Research Conference at UC Davis. The abstract was also published in the abstract book. I earned a certificate for outstanding academic achievement in the presentation of scholarly research.

The poster, abstract, and certificate are below.
